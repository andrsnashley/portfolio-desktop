About Me
Welcome to my site! My name is Ashley, and I'm currently a third year Computer Science & Information Analysis major at the University of Michigan. My work and interests focuses on Human Computer Interaction and Data Analysis. I'm not only passionate about data and technology, but I'm also very interested in studying how people interact with technology.
My previous experience and work is mostly focused in software engineering, project management, and data analysis. This past summer, I worked as a software engineering intern at Learning A-Z, where I designed interactive classroom pages for students and programmed student-avatar capabilities for the website’s class chart pages. I additionally worked with programming and data analysis as a project manager under the University of Michigan’s Psychology Department where I ran my own experiments on how the spread of information in social networks leads to network clusters. I also had the opportunity to work as a research fellow under the Intel Semiconductor Research Corporation Fellowship where I mined data from electronic textbook log files to study student behavior on computing problems and tests.
